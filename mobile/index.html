Hi!

My name is Brecht.
I'm currently pursuing a PhD in audio engineering at the Centre for Digital Music, part of the School of Electronic Engineering and Computer Science at Queen Mary University of London. I published and presented research on perception of recording and mixing engineering, intelligent audio effects and mixing systems, and analysis of music production practices. 

Prior to this, I received a BSc and MSc in Electronic Engineering at the University of Ghent (Universiteit Gent) in Belgium, during which I also studied at the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology in Zürich (Eidgenössische Technologische Hochschule Zürich) in Switzerland for one year at MSc level. 
(Here's my CV!)
I am a recording and mixing engineer, in and around London, UK. 

I play the piano (jazz and pop) both in bands and solo. Please refer to the Contact section if you're looking for musical accompaniment for an event. 

I teach piano, audio engineering and music production. All levels (beginner to advanced), all genres (from classical via jazz over pop to electronic music). 

An active member of the Audio Engineering Society, I am currently the chair of its Student Delegate Assembly for Europe and International Regions, committee member of the British Section and the chair of the London UK Student Section. I also chaired the 2015 edition of the annual AES student event Up Your Output! at SAE London and remain active in its organising committee. 

Please check 'Music' for a very limited selection of musical works and productions I've been involved in, 'Blog' for a weblog about my research and other activities, 'Publications' for an overview of my published work, 'Software' for development work available online, and see 'Contact' to enquire about hiring, lessons, collaborations or a cup of coffee.
